<b>we are sheep</b>

We do not live in a democracy.

I'm not saying that for shock value, it's really true if you interpret political theory literally.  What we have here is called an <i>electoral republic</i>.  Kids, can you say "electoral republic"?  I knew you could.

For those of you who aren't appreciating that distinction, i'll explain.  The rest of you can skip to the next paragraph.  An electoral republic is a politcal state where the populace is governed by a selected body of people who make decisions on national policies and pass laws & legislation.  That body of people may or may not be selected directly by the populace.  In the example of the US, the governing people are selected according to a party system which is in turn balanced by the voting of the populace.  Had this been a democracy, the general populace would have some flavor of direct control over policies and legislation.  Have you participated in any referendums lately?  No?  In my opinion it's probably better that way.

We do not live in an electoral republic.  We are impotent proles.

"What?  But you just said..." Yeah i know, but <a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2000/12/13/politics/13SCOT.html" target="link">according to the supreme court</a> of the united states (the one largely apointed by George H. W. Bush and his predecessor Rambo Ronnie the Ray-Gun) our votes don't directly determine the balance of the electorate, ergo we don't influence the selection of those in charge of policy making and legislation.

I know what you're thinking.  Yes I do too!  Fine, you don't have to believe me, but check this out. WHAT THE HELL ARE YOU THINKING?  WE CAN'T LET THIS HAPPEN?  C'MON YOU CANDYASS DEMOCRATS, VOTE NAY!  NAY!!!  <b>NAY!!!</b>".  *sigh*
