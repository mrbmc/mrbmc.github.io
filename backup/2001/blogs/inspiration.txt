<b>Inspiration</b>

Left shoulder itches.

Went on a bender this weekend, imbibed myself with excessive amounts of animal tranquilizer.  I am an animal of sorts, yes, but oddly enough it didn't make me feel more tranquil.  Huh....  Sufficeth to say, productivity was hampered.  In place of productivity, I found a good deal of inspiration.

And that's really the cream of it isn't it, the inspiration that is.   It's the cream of life's bounty.  It's really that intellectual intervention of the unknown or the divine that motivates and drives me to continue the path.  That instant of creation in my mind that blocks the memory and view of all the slings and arrows of outrageous fortune.  Inspiration is powerful, I think I might live only by inspiration and penne al'pomodoro for the rest of my days and still die a content man.

Someday we'll be able to instantiate or dowload nowledge directly into our consciousness, maybe.  But the joy of learning and knowledge is the process of discovery.  Simply donwloading Hamlet won't give you the pleasure of revealing metaphor by metaphor the immediate experience of connecting context with experience.  Maybe you could download another person's experience of enjoying Shakespeare, or download ancient Greek so as to appreciaate Homer in his native Toungue (wouldn't Thoreau be proud).  Perhaps we will have the restraint for compromise.  Knowledge is a treaure because it is rare and must be earned.  If it becomes a commodity, like information and data, easily and readily obtained in quantities beyond our abilities of absorbtion, where will our impulse to create new knowledge come from?
