<b>karma and fate and dumb ass shit luck</b>

Karma and luck move in fickle shifts throught the universe.  It's arbitrary, but fascinating and compelling.

I found a palm pilot in a cab yesterday.  I tried to discover the identity of the owner and contact them, but to no avail... Now i have a free palm pilot.  Funny how objects drift in and out of people's lives.  This is fortunate for me, but i have a pang of regret knowing that this same stroke of luck is bad for someone else.  Or are the fates more just and fair than that?  Would those fabled dames architect this transition as beneficial for the previous owner?  Maybe they didn't want or need or buy this object.  Maybe it isn't a loss for them, but unencumbering them.  Why would one person arbitrarily benefit at another person's loss?  Maybe they deserve it.  *shrug*  we'll never know....

I had some misfortune too.  Tonight i was burned on a deal.  It's apparently easy enough to pull out the ketaset and replace it with water.  I'm more sanguine about this development though; you buy enough drugs for long enough you're bound to be ripped off sooner or later.  "beshert" as the say in Israel.  I wasn't meant to obtain this vertrinary supply.  timing...

Maybe nothing is really beneficial or detrimental to us, some things just happen and it's up to our perception to put the positive or negative spin on events.  I suppose with the wrong chemical imbalance in our heads, the whole world and all its tragedy could be good luck to us and warm tidings.  Actually I think we measure our place in the world by the nature and volume of our misery, but hey... maybe we could all take a federally subsidized percription pharmecutical... a fucking drug by any other name... and everybody will have good luck.

ok... i'm running dry on insight here, so i'll leave it at that.
