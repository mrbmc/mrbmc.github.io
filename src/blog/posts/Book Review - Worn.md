When you buy a product for a price that is lower than reasonable, ask yourself how that's possible. A drop in value doesn't happen without a corresponding effect somewhere else. Someone, somewhere, is paying for that discount. 

When you buy a cotton t-shirt for $5, someone is subsidizing that low price. Maybe it's the migrant workers harvesting the cotton, the factory laborers assembling the shirt, the taxpayers supporting asymmetrical trade regulations, or future generations cleaning up the environment from unscrupulous fabric dying or weed control dumping toxic waste water. Maybe it's all of those factors to get you that deal.

What's the cost of supporting corporate puppet regimes in Guatemala and Honduras that force people to flee from indentured servitude, often migrating illegally to the US where they die while crossing the deadly Sonoran desert to avoid border patrols?
