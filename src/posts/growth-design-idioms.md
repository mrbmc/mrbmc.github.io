I've seen a few recurring themes emerge over 15+ years of working on "Growth Design" for Disney, MLB, BlogTalkRadio, et al. These nuggets are fortune cookie length to get attention but there is an evidential story behind each one.

----

1. "Growth" is a business goal. You need to work harder to be user-centered with a business-centered team brand.
1. ```Conversion = Motivation / Friction```
1. The biggest design challenge of Growth Design is communicating value.
1. “We Buy On Emotion and Justify with Logic.”
1. You can easily grow horizontally or vertically, but not both.
3. For every basis change in conversion rate there is an opposite but equal change in LTV.
1. Nobody walks on the lot who doesn’t want to buy.
1. A leaky payment screen means there's a problem with your offer.
3. There’s no such thing as giving prospects too much information about your product.
4. Organic user growth is like organic food. It's more expensive but more sustainable.
5. Velocity is not a KPI.
